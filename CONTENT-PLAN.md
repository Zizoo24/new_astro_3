## Why the Old Plan Failed

The January 2026 plan targeted generic keywords: "legal translation dubai" (position 70), "translation services dubai" (position 21). Research revealed why this can't work:

- **Al Alsun** has 350+ blog posts across 5 domains targeting these exact terms
- **CLT** has operated since 1996, **AGATO** for 20+ years
- **15+ competitors** fight for "legal translation dubai"
- We have 18 blog posts. We cannot win a volume war.

**What we're stopping:**
- Chasing "legal translation dubai" and "translation services dubai" head terms
- Publishing 2 generic blog posts per week
- Writing about "why choose us" or "top translation services"
- Competing on content volume

---

## The Strategy: Own the Journey, Not the Keyword

Every person who needs a translation is in the middle of a larger process: getting a work permit, applying for Golden Visa, licensing as a healthcare professional, settling an estate. Translation is step 3 of 7.

**Al Alsun's game:** Rank for "translation services Dubai" (generic, brutal competition)
**Our game:** Rank for the entire journey where translation is a required step

**Why this works:**
- Zero translation companies create authority-specific process content (validated across 40+ searches)
- aburuf.ae proved the model: one ADJD will registration guide ranks them for will translation
- Translayte.com (UK) proved it for MOHRE: one work visa guide outranks Dubai-based companies
- OnlineTranslation.ae already ranks for MOFA attestation content — we've done this before

**Our unfair advantage:**
Process knowledge. We know what GDRFA's system actually checks, how DHA DataFlow connects to residency, why MOHRE rejects applications. Competitors write about themselves ("500+ linguists"). We write about what happens inside government systems. That knowledge is the moat.

## The 4 Pillars

### Pillar 1: Authority Process Guides

"How to get your [document] accepted by [authority]"

Not "we translate for MOHRE." Instead: "How to get your work permit through MOHRE — complete document guide." Captures people BEFORE they search for "translation." Our deep knowledge of GDRFA, DHA, MOFA is the moat.

### Pillar 2: Rejection Prevention

"Why [authority] rejected your application"

Only someone with operational experience can write this. It's unfakeable. This is the content people search for at 11pm in a panic — high intent, zero competition. Across 40+ searches, no translation company has built rejection-prevention as a content category.

### Pillar 3: Decision Content (Right-Selling)

"When you DON'T need MOJ certification — and save AED 150"

Contrarian. Every competitor says "buy everything." We say "here's what you actually need." Builds trust, gets shared, earns links naturally. OnlineTranslation.ae's existing MOJ vs Certified post is already the best decision-making piece found across all searches.

### Pillar 4: Nationality-Specific Guides

"Indian degree holders: MOHRE work permit translation guide"

People search by their situation, not by "translation." 1.45 million Bangladeshi and Egyptian expats have virtually zero nationality-specific document content targeting them. Each guide naturally incorporates Hague Convention status — our verified expertise.

## Execution Phases

### Phase 1: Rejection Prevention + Decision Tree (Weeks 1-3)

**Why first:** Zero competition. High intent. High conversion. People searching "visa rejected" or "document rejected" need help NOW.

| # | Content | Type | URL | Target Query |
|---|---------|------|-----|--------------|
| 1 | "GDRFA visa rejected? Document problems that cause delays and how to fix them" | Blog | `/blog/gdrfa-visa-document-problems/` | gdrfa visa rejected documents, visa rejection UAE translation |
| 2 | "MOHRE work permit rejected: common document issues employees miss" | Blog | `/blog/mohre-work-permit-document-issues/` | work permit rejected UAE, MOHRE document requirements |
| 3 | "What translation type do I need? MOJ vs Certified vs Notarized — by authority" | Resource | `/resources/translation-type-decision-guide/` | what translation do I need UAE, MOJ vs certified |

**Content approach:**
- Frame around the PROBLEM (rejection, delay, confusion), not the service (translation)
- Include specific authority portal screenshots or step references where possible
- End with "how proper translation prevents this" — natural service connection
- Each piece 2,000-3,000 words following CLAUDE.md structure

**Internal linking:**
- Link FROM existing service pages (birth certificate, PCC, degree) INTO these guides
- Link FROM these guides TO specific document translation pages
- Update hub pages to reference these as "before you submit" resources

## Content Specifications

### Blog Posts (Pillar 2 + 3)

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Length | 2,000-3,000 words |
| Angle | Problem-first (rejection, confusion, cost) — NOT service-first |
| Structure | H2 every 200-300 words, bullet lists, numbered steps |
| Schema | Article + FAQ (5-8 questions) |
| Internal Links | 8+ to service pages and resource guides |
| CTA | "Not sure? WhatsApp your document" — mid-article + end |
| Voice | Calm, knowledgeable, honest. See CLAUDE.md Part VI. |

### Resource Guides (Pillar 1 + 4)

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Length | 2,500-4,000 words |
| Angle | Process-first — translation is one step in a larger journey |
| Structure | Numbered steps, document checklists, authority-specific sections |
| Schema | HowTo + FAQ (8-15 questions) + BreadcrumbList |
| Internal Links | 10+ cross-linking to service pages, other guides, blog posts |
| CTA | Contextual within process: "Step 4 requires certified translation — WhatsApp your document" |

## Content Quality Checklist

Before publishing any content, verify:

- [ ] Sentence length: Max 25 words (target 15-20)
- [ ] Paragraph length: Max 3-4 sentences
- [ ] No hype words: "best", "leading", "#1", "revolutionary"
- [ ] Hague Convention facts: UAE is NOT a member
- [ ] MOJ License #701 mentioned where relevant
- [ ] WhatsApp CTA: +971 50 862 0217
- [ ] Schema markup: FAQ at minimum, HowTo for process guides
- [ ] Internal links: 8+ per blog, 10+ per resource guide
- [ ] Mobile-friendly: Short paragraphs, clear headers
- [ ] "Right-selling" check: Does this content tell readers what they DON'T need?
- [ ] Authority references: Specific portal names (ICP, Amer, Tasheel, etc.)
- [ ] Year in title or content: "2026" for freshness signal

## Content Publishing Checklist (Post-Creation)

After creating any new content, complete ALL of these steps:

### 1. Internal Linking (CRITICAL)

**Outbound Links (from new page):**
- [ ] Add 8+ links to relevant service pages
- [ ] Include links to related resources/guides
- [ ] Link to attestation pages when document authentication is mentioned
- [ ] Link to authority guides when cross-referencing processes

**Inbound Links (to new page):**
- [ ] Update `src/data/serviceLinks.ts` with new page entry
- [ ] Add links from 3-5 existing related pages
- [ ] Update hub page if content fits a category

### 2. Blog Index Update

For blog posts:
- [ ] Add entry to `posts` array in `src/pages/blog/index.astro`
- [ ] Include: slug, title, excerpt, date, category, categoryLabel, readTime

### 3. Search Index Update

- [ ] Add entry to `public/scripts/search-index.js`
- [ ] Add entry to `scripts/generate-search-index.js`

### 4. Navigation Check

For major new sections:
- [ ] Update `src/data/navigation.ts` if navigation should include the page
- [ ] Add to mainNav, mobileNav, and mobileNavHubs as appropriate

### 5. Verification

- [ ] Run `npm run build` to verify no errors
- [ ] Check all internal links work
- [ ] Verify schema markup renders correctly
